Advanced Soldering Techniques

Dive deeper into soldering skills for Surface Mount Devices (SMD), reflow, and professional repair workflows.

SMD (Surface Mount Device) Soldering

Surface Mount soldering is a higher‑precision technique for tiny components attached directly to PCB pads rather than through holes. It requires finer tips, flux, and often magnification. 0

Desoldering Techniques

Removing solder or components cleanly is a critical advanced skill. Use desoldering braid (wick) or a suction pump, especially when correcting mistakes or replacing parts. 2

Watch: Professional Desoldering Methods

This video shows advanced desoldering tricks used by repair pros to remove excess solder without damaging pads or components. 3

Hot Air Reflow & Rework

For more complex PCB repair like removing QFPs, BGA, or low‑profile SMT packages, a hot air station is invaluable. Heat is applied evenly using controlled airflow and temperature profiles. 4

Watch: Hot Air Rework Demo

Hot air rework helps melt solder paste evenly and remove tiny devices without lifting pads or traces — essential for advanced repairs. 5

Fine‑Pitch & Multi‑Lead Components

When dealing with fine‑pitch ICs (e.g., QFP, SOIC), use a flat or bevel tip, flux generously, and solder from one lead to the next while melting solder with minimal movement. Precision and preparation are key. 6

Pro Tip: Cleaning pads with isopropyl alcohol and applying fresh flux before soldering improves wetting and reduces bridging. 7
