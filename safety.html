Soldering Safety Tips

Protect your eyes, skin, and lungs while soldering by following these essential safety measures.

Eye Protection

Always wear safety glasses or goggles to prevent solder splashes from injuring your eyes.

Video Demo

Respiratory Safety

Solder fumes can be harmful. Always solder in a well-ventilated area or use a fume extractor to remove smoke and fumes from your workspace.

Video Demo

Hand & Skin Protection

Wear heat-resistant gloves if needed, and never touch the hot iron or molten solder. Keep your workspace organized to avoid accidents.

Workspace Safety

Use a heat-resistant mat or work surface, and keep flammable materials away. Always place your soldering iron in a proper stand.

  • Organize your tools to avoid accidental burns.
  • Keep a first-aid kit nearby for minor burns.
  • Work in a well-lit area to see your joints clearly.
